Getting Started

The ubiquity of data today enables investors to make better investment decisions. However, sometimes financial data is not easily accessible to all investors, and advanced investing analytics tools can be very expensive.
STOCKS & Sentiments is a tool that provides insights from stock market time-series data of US-based public companies and how they correlate with the news that surround them every day.

Whether you are an experienced investor or you are venturing in this field for the first time, we hope that STOCKS & Sentiments can help guide your investment decisions in the stock market by leveraging news sentiment information.

Defining the Users, Data & Tasks

Users

This site is built for both seasoned and new equity investors. Are you an investor interested in understanding how different stocks perform and how their performance correlates with news sentiment? You are in the right place!

Data

We will be using two sets of data from a current Kaggle competition hosted by Two Sigma. The first is financial stock market data with daily time series of prices and returns from 2007 to 2016 provided by Intrinion, and the second is news and sentiment data provided by Thomson Reuters for the same time period.



Tasks

# 1

Exploring stock market performance and news volume & sentiment of different companies over time by visualizing time-series data.

# 2

Gaining insights into which companies are the most susceptible to price fluctuations due to news sentiment, and which are not.

# 3

Using our knowledge and visualization tools to build a sentiment resilient and high performance portfolio.

First let's try to chart a company's stock price and news sentiment

Using the drop-down menu below, select any company that you would like to explore. Next, move the slider bar to indicate the time frame that interests you. You can now see the stock price movement and the number of news articles that have been published about this company.

Based on the content of the news, we classify the sentiment: is it negative or positive news? To assist with this, we created a "Sentiment Rating" metric which transforms the sentiment analysis probability of whether a news article is postive, neutral or negative into a single averaged daily rating between -1 for negative and 1 for positive.

Which companies are most influenced by sentiment?

We created a metric "Sentiment Influence" to measure the ratio of a company's stock price return over the Sentiment Rating. Now we can rank the companies across the entire time period by Sentiment Influence.

The below view shows a ranking of companies that are highly influenced by sentiment (i.e. Sentiment Influence > 1) from the most to the least influenced.

Companies that have high sentiment influence and low returns, may signal highly speculative companies which have large swings in daily trading which correlates to news sentiment, and yet, over the entire period, do not have high returns.

How about companies which are not influenced by news sentiment?

We now take a closer look into companies that have Sentiment Influence < 1 and rank them from highest to lowest performance measured by their total returns over the entire time period.

These companies could deserve a place in a sentiment resilient portfolio.

Now, how can we use what we learned on a portfolio level?

We are now moving to porfolio building and it would be helpful to map out the investment returns against the Sentiment Ratings for a group of companies.

Pick out your portfolio, your current one or your dream one. Let us see how the companies that you have chosen will match up with one another, and also evaluate the average Sentiment Influence of the entire portfolio.
